## Round 1: Grammar Checking — Who's More Accurate?

This is the foundation of both tools. But real testing tells a surprising story.

### Grammarly's Grammar Check
Grammarly detects up to 250+ types of grammar, punctuation, and spelling errors. It doesn't just fix the mistake — it explains *why* the correction improves your sentence, which helps you actually learn and improve over time.

It particularly excels at:
* **Tone inconsistency** — e.g., "this email sounds too passive-aggressive"
* **Clarity issues** — "this sentence is too long, split it"
* **Vocabulary suggestions** — replacing weak words with stronger alternatives
* **Context-aware corrections** — understanding what you meant to say vs what you typed

In multiple independent tests, Grammarly detected significantly more errors than QuillBot in formal English writing. One test found Grammarly catching 30 errors in a document where QuillBot only caught 13.

### QuillBot's Grammar Check
QuillBot's grammar checker is solid but not as deep. It catches the obvious errors — spelling, basic grammar, punctuation — but misses subtle issues like tense inconsistency, misplaced modifiers, and overly wordy phrasing that Grammarly flags.

However, in one independent test by Scribbr, QuillBot surprisingly identified all 20 deliberate errors in a test document — while Grammarly only caught 11. QuillBot also performs notably better with British English variants.

Another 2026 test showed both tools detecting 8 errors in the same document — tied. The difference was in the quality of the suggestions, where Grammarly's contextual fixes were more precise.

**🏆 Grammar Winner: Grammarly (for everyday writing)**
For professional emails, blog posts, and business documents, Grammarly's real-time contextual grammar checking is more reliable and educational. QuillBot's grammar checker is a competent secondary tool but not a primary solution.
