## Round 1: Grammar Checking — Who's More Accurate?

This is the foundation of both tools. But real testing tells a surprising story.

### Grammarly's Grammar Check
Grammarly detects up to 250+ types of grammar, punctuation, and spelling errors. It doesn't just fix the mistake — it explains *why* the correction improves your sentence, which helps you actually learn and improve over time.

It particularly excels at:
* **Tone inconsistency** — e.g., "this email sounds too passive-aggressive"
* **Clarity issues** — "this sentence is too long, split it"
* **Vocabulary suggestions** — replacing weak words with stronger alternatives

### QuillBot's Grammar Check
QuillBot's grammar checker is solid but not as deep. It catches the obvious errors — spelling, basic grammar, punctuation — but misses subtle issues like tense inconsistency that Grammarly flags. However, QuillBot also performs notably better with British English variants.

**🏆 Grammar Winner: Grammarly (for everyday writing)**

## Round 2: Paraphrasing — No Contest

This is QuillBot's home turf — and Grammarly doesn't even try to compete seriously.

### QuillBot Paraphrasing
QuillBot offers 7 different paraphrasing modes:
* **Standard** — balanced rewrite
* **Fluency** — focus on natural flow
* **Formal** — professional tone
* **Simple** — easier language
* **Creative** — more unique phrasing
* **Expand** — makes text longer
* **Shorten** — condenses text

The **Synonym Slider** lets you control how aggressively words are changed. You can also use **Freeze Words** to lock keywords in place (critical for SEO bloggers and content marketers — your target keyword stays exactly as written).

### Grammarly Paraphrasing
Grammarly does offer AI rewriting suggestions, but they're limited to sentence-level refinements — "make this more concise" or "make this more confident." It's a helpful polish tool, not a paraphrasing engine.

**🏆 Paraphrasing Winner: QuillBot — by a mile**
